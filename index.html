HPDB

Database of Hadamard Products of Modified Hypergeometric Functions

This is a database of certain interesting Hadamard products of two modified Hypergeometric $_2F_1$ functions $f_{k,r,s}$, which is defined as:

$$f_{k,r,s}(z)=(1-z)^k{_2F_1}\left({r,s\atop 1}; z\right).$$

We made a rather incomplete search of Hadamard products $(k_1,r_1,s_1)\star(k_2,r_2,s_2)$ such that when $z=1$ it has certain interesting supercongruence properties: That is to say, if we denote $f_{k_i,r_i,s_i}=\sum_{n=0}^\infty f_{n,i}z^i$, then we find that the following truncated power series modulo $p^2$ is sometimes related to some modular form:

$$\sum_{n=0}^{p-1}f_{n,1}g_{n,1}\cdot 1^n\equiv a_p(k_1,r_1,s_1,k_2,r_2,s_2)\bmod p^2.$$

For example, if we consider the datum $(0,r_1,s_1,0,r_2,s_2)$, then this Hadamard product could be simply written as a $_4F_3$ generalized hypergeometric function:

$${_2F_1}\left({r_1,s_1\atop 1}; z\right)\star{_2F_1}\left({r_2,s_2\atop 1}; z\right)={_4F_3}\left({r_1,s_1,r_2,s_2\atop 1,1,1}; z\right).$$

For some choices $(r_1,s_1,r_2,s_2)$ like $(\frac 14,\frac12,\frac 12,\frac 34)$, this is corresponded to a one parameter hypergeometric Calabi--Yau threefold family $X_{4,2}(1^6)$ (complete intersection of a quartic and a quardric in $\mathbb P^5(1,1,1,1,1,1)$). The conifold fibre $z=1$ has smooth rigid Calabi--Yau threefold resolutions, so the supercongruences come from the modularity of rigid Calabi--Yau threefolds. In the $(\frac14,\frac 12,\frac12,\frac34)$ case, using LMFDB labels, the modular form is $\mathsf{16.4.a.a}=\mathsf{8.4.a.a}\otimes\chi_{-4}$. This time we have:

$$a_p\left(0,\frac14,\frac12,0,\frac34,\frac12\right)\equiv a_p(\mathsf{16.4.a.a})\bmod p^2$$

How to Use This Database

  • Datum: Firstly, the tuple $(k_1,r_1,s_1,k_2,r_2,s_2)$ is called the datum.
  • Series Expansion: For each datum we compute series expansion to $z^5$, that is $F=1+f_1z^1+\cdots+f_5z^5+O(z^6)$.
  • Equivalence Classes: For each expansion we collect all datums with this expansion, since they have the same supercongruence property.
  • Prime Computations: We compute $a_p$ for prime $p$ from $5$ to $199$.
  • Labels: The old label is a short label to describe the $|a_p|$ series (the minimal absolute residue, $-(p^2-1)/2 < a_p \le (p^2+1)/2$, and we take the absolute value $0 \le |a_p| \le (p^2+1)/2$). Usually we use LMFDB labels or something like $\mathsf{p*(1or5)}$ (this means $|a_p|$ equiv $p$ or $5p$ modulo $p^2$).
  • Picard-Fuchs Operator: We also compute the minimal order Picard--Fuchs operator (PFO) of the Hadamard product power series $D(F(z))=0$. The operator $D$ is written as a polynomial in $z$ and $t=z\frac{d}{dz}$.
  • Degree Notation: We call the polynomial degree of $t,z$ in $D$ the $t$-degree and $z$-degree respectively.
  • Discriminant: The discriminant of $D$ is the coefficient (as a polynomial of $z$) of the highest power $t^{\text{tdeg}}$.
  • Riemann Symbol: We also compute the Riemann symbol of $D$ at rational or quadratic algebraic number of $z$.
